Americans will casually greet you and ask, “How are you doing?” A good way to respond would be with “fine”, “great”, “I’m good, how are you?” or “Very well, thank you!”

Tasnima and Ingrid | Graphic Design

Sometimes you shake hands with someone you meet for the first time. Don’t be offended when people say, “See you later,” even though you may end up never seeing them again—it’s just an expression. We usually don’t hug people either when we greet each other for the first time; maybe after spending a few hours together, you can give a casual hug. In class, teachers will encourage you to speak up in critique or volunteer for demonstrations. Class participation is a huge part of American education.

Kendra and Joseph | Graphic Design

This may be difficult at first, but practice makes perfect, and it will get easier as you become more familiar with your peers.
