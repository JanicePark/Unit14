Finding work in the United States is a complex process for international students. Before starting any application, make sure that you hold on to your I-20 Form
  • It identifies your program of study and status as an F-1 Visa student.
  • You will need to obtain a signature from OISS each time you travel in and out of the country.
  • Many applications also require I-94
    I-94 application form
     your arrival and departure record, so make sure to print updated versions.
#BecomingEligibleforwork You need to apply for a Social Security Number, which is equivalent to a national identity number for U.S. residents. This process takes 2—6 weeks. Follow these steps before working:
  • First, obtain a letter from your employer and submit it to the OISS, which will give you another letter on department letterhead.
  • Prepare your I-20, I-94 or passport admission stamp, F-1 Student Visa, the social security application form
  • Copy both letters
  • submit these to the nearest Social Security Office in Providence.
  • You will also have to go through an interview. After the process is complete, your card will be ready in about 2 weeks.
#StudentPayroll If your job is on-campus or part of work-study, contact financial aid to enroll in the student payroll system. On Wa.risd.edu, you clock in your times to get paid.
#Internships #CPT #OPT Start applying as early as December or January for summer internships. As an international student, you will need to apply for Curricular Practical Training (CPT),
CPT Application Form
 which authorizes a total of 12 months of work over 4 years in your program of study. You will first need to register your internship on Artworks (Checkbox icon - link to Artworks) to obtain departmental approval for credit. After that, prepare photocopies of your passport, F-1 Student Visa, I-20 form, I-94 form, and a letter from your employer, and submit this to the OISS either personally or online (link to CPT application checklist). Processing of the request usually takes 3-5 days.
#After RISD To start work after graduating from RISD, you will need to apply for Optional Practical Training (OPT), which permits 12 months of work in the United States after completion of your degree. The best time to apply for OPT is close to graduation in your senior year. Your job will have to begin within 60 days after program completion, and you must apply for all of the 12-month benefit available. Submit your application to the OISS either personally or online (link to OPT application checklist). Processing of the request usually takes 3-5 days.
While the process may seem daunting and it is natural to feel frustrated, don’t stress out too much about it! Start early, give the applications ample time, and anticipate unexpected events so that the process runs smoothly. If you’re confused, check in with the OISS at Carr Haus and feel free to talk to your seniors! Many of them are international and have overcome the same issues. Working in the United States is an enriching experience and adds a colorful chapter to your college life as an international student. Good luck!
